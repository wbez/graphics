Who decides what books are banned in prison?

The decision comes down to a publication review officer, the facility’s chief adminstrative officer, and sometimes, a committee, according to documents obtained from the Illinois Department of Corrections.

If that staffer thinks it’s questionable, the inmate is notified and can ask for a review.

A publication arrives at the prison.

A staffer looks at it to see if it’s something that could be considered questionable material.

Obscene?

Encourages or instructs criminal activity?

Sexually explicit material that poses a threat?

A publication review officer then decides if it should be banned based on a long list of criteria.

That decision is sometimes reviewed by a comittee. Final approval of the ban comes from the chief administrative officer.

If it’s banned, the title is added to the list, and stays there for three years.
