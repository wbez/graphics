How To Get A Concealed Carry License In Illinois

In Illinois, police departments can try to stop a person from legally carrying a gun if officers have a “reasonable suspicion” that person could be a danger to themselves or others. But it’s the only state where a review board can rule that person eligible for a gun license anyway.

After getting a Firearm Owners Identification Card, you can apply through the Illinois State Police.

All state law enforcement agencies have access to the database. Some, like the Cook County Sherriff’s Office, check it every day.

While state police conduct a background check, your application goes into a database.
